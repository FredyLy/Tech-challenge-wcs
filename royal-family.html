Our Royal Family members

King Aeetes

Aeëtes was the son of Sun god Helios and the Oceanid Perseis, brother of Circe, Perses and Pasiphaë, and father of Medea, Chalciope and Absyrtus.
His consorts was either Idyia, the youngest daughter of Oceanus,Asterodeia, a Caucasian Oceanid, the Nereid Neaera, Clytia, Ipsia or Eurylyte.

According to others, he was the brother of Perses, a king of Tauris, husband of his niece Hecate, and father of Medea, Chalciope and Absyrtus. Yet other versions make Aeëtes a native of Corinth and son of Ephyra, an Oceanid, or else of a certain Antiope.
Asterope was also one of the possible mothers of Aeëtes.

Source: Wikipédia

Queen Idyia

In Greek mythology, Idyia or Eidyia was a daughter of Oceanus and Tethys, and queen to Aeëtes, king of Colchis.

She was the mother of Medea, Chalciope and Absyrtus. According to Apollonius of Rhodes, she was the youngest of the Oceanides.

Source: Wikipédia

Princess Medea

In Greek mythology, Medea is the daughter of King Aeëtes of Colchis, a niece of Circe and the granddaughter of the sun god Helios.

Medea figures in the myth of Jason and the Argonauts, appearing in Hesiod's Theogony around 700 BC, but best known from Euripides's tragedy Medea and Apollonius of Rhodes' epic Argonautica.

Medea is known in most stories as a sorceress and is often depicted as a priestess of the goddess Hecate.

Source: Wikipédia
